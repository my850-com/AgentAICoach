Leonardo AI Image Creation Test

Tested Leonardo.ai to create a simple set of images for a YouTube channel. What we learned: AI content safety filters are sensitive and specific, even for seemingly innocent requests. Understanding content flags saves time and credits.

The Test

We wanted to see how quickly Leonardo.ai could generate complete YouTube channel artwork - banner, profile picture, and thumbnails - using a single natural language prompt.

The Prompt

"Create all necessary artwork for my YouTube channel making sure it fits with YouTube recommended sizes and banner guides"

The Result

Content Safety Block

Failed Generation
Your request did not meet content safety guidelines. Your tokens have been credited back to your account.

What Happened

Leonardo's content safety filter flagged the request. While asking for YouTube channel artwork seems harmless, the platform's AI likely interpreted "create all necessary artwork" or the combination of terms as potentially problematic for one of these reasons:

  • Vague commercial intent: "YouTube channel" without context could trigger brand/content safety filters
  • All-encompassing language: "All necessary artwork" suggests bulk generation which some platforms restrict
  • Platform name mentions: Some AI tools restrict generating content specifically for named platforms

The Lesson

AI image generators have strict content filters. Even innocent prompts can trip safety guidelines. The good news: Leonardo immediately refunded the tokens, so we learned this lesson for free.

Better Prompt Strategy

Instead of asking for "all channel artwork," break it into specific, individual prompts:

"Create a professional banner image for a real estate education channel, 2560x1440 resolution, clean modern design with blue and gold colors"

This is specific, avoids platform names, and requests a single deliverable.
